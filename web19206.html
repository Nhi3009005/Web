The area displaying Do paper at the Vietnam Literature Museum is a poetic space, honoring the traditional material that has long been associated with the writing culture and calligraphy of the nation. Here, visitors can admire carefully preserved handmade Do paper sheets, used for copying poems, writing, printing books or calligraphy. The space is gently decorated, with warm lighting, highlighting each paper fiber, each trace of time left on the surface. In addition to the artifacts, the area also introduces the traditional Do paper making process, from selecting Do tree bark, soaking, pounding, filtering powder to the finished product. This is not only a place to preserve ancient materials, but also a bridge between literary heritage and the quintessence of Vietnamese handicrafts, bringing a profound experience to lovers of literature and national culture.
